# Interview 2: Requirements Gathering Interview with Head Coach

**Interviewee:** Mr. Dileep Jayasuriya (Head Coach – Roar Fitness)
**Interviewer:** Scrum Master (Software Development Company)
**Purpose:** To gather operational requirements related to instructor management, workout sessions, and member training activities for the proposed Roar Fitness ERP System.

---

**Scrum Master:** Good morning, Mr. Dileep. Thank you for taking the time to participate in this interview. We are gathering information about the current operations of Roar Fitness to understand the requirements for developing the new ERP system.

**Head Coach:** Good morning. You're welcome. I'm happy to provide the necessary information to support the development of the system.

---

**Scrum Master:** Could you briefly explain your responsibilities as the Head Coach and how instructors currently manage their daily activities?

**Head Coach:** My main responsibilities include managing the coaching team, monitoring trainer activities, creating workout plans, supervising member progress, and ensuring members receive proper guidance. Currently, most of these activities are managed manually through communication, spreadsheets, and separate records. Instructors coordinate with members by phone or in person, and there is no single platform where the coaching team can view assignments, requests, and schedules together.

---

**Scrum Master:** How are members currently assigned to instructors or personal training sessions?

**Head Coach:** Usually, member assignments and personal training schedules are managed manually. The management team decides the assignments, and instructors communicate with members directly regarding their sessions. For workout and meal plans, members often approach reception or speak to a trainer, and the details are recorded informally. A centralized system would make this process more organized and easier to manage.

---

**Scrum Master:** What challenges do instructors face when managing member workout plans and progress tracking?

**Head Coach:** The main challenge is maintaining accurate records of member progress, workout plans, and previous training details. Since information is stored separately, it can be difficult to quickly access a member's history and provide personalized recommendations. When a member changes instructor or requests a new plan, we sometimes lose continuity because earlier notes are not always available in one place.

---

**Scrum Master:** What features would you expect from the ERP system to support instructors and improve training management?

**Head Coach:** The system should allow instructors to manage assigned members, review workout and meal plan requests, create and update approved plans, view member information, and track member progress over time. Instructors should also be able to propose VIP or personal training sessions and see their general class timetable. This would help trainers provide better services and maintain organized records.

---

**Scrum Master:** How would you like the system to manage VIP or personal training sessions?

**Head Coach:** VIP sessions should allow instructors to propose sessions with details such as class type, studio, date, time, capacity, and fee. Administrators should review and accept or reject these proposals before they are published. Once accepted, sessions should be visible on the public schedule and available for member enrollment. Members who enroll should complete payment online so that attendance and revenue are properly recorded. Instructors should be able to view their assigned sessions, member details, and session history through their dashboard.

---

**Scrum Master:** How should general group classes be handled in the proposed system?

**Head Coach:** General classes such as cardio, yoga, Zumba, and strength training should be managed centrally by administration. Admin staff should create and maintain the weekly timetable with instructor assignment, studio, duration, and class category. Members and the public should be able to view the timetable to plan their visits. At the initial stage, general classes can be for schedule visibility rather than seat booking, as our priority is to display accurate class information across the website and member portal.

---

**Scrum Master:** Would integrating attendance information into the system be useful for trainers?

**Head Coach:** Yes. Having access to member attendance records would help instructors understand member consistency and provide better guidance. Integration with the biometric attendance system would provide accurate attendance information without manual tracking. Members and instructors should enter the gym through fingerprint scan, and the system should record whether access was granted or denied based on membership status and account condition. Instructors should also be able to view their own attendance history.

---

**Scrum Master:** What should happen when a member's membership expires or when management needs to restrict an account?

**Head Coach:** If a membership expires, the member should still be able to sign in but should see a clear renewal message. Features such as plan requests, VIP session enrollment, and profile updates should be disabled until the membership is renewed. Both online renewal and front-desk renewal should be supported. If management restricts a member account, future login should be blocked and fingerprint access should be denied. If a member is already logged in when restricted, the portal should show a warning and prevent further actions until they contact reception.

---

**Scrum Master:** What role should the public website play in supporting coaching operations?

**Head Coach:** The public website should promote Roar Fitness and connect visitors to the ERP system. It should display membership plans, the general and VIP class timetable, active instructors with photos and qualifications, and a contact form for inquiries. Visitors who wish to join should be able to select a package, register, and complete membership payment online. Accurate instructor profiles on the website would help members choose the right trainer when submitting a plan request.

---

**Scrum Master:** What type of reports or information would be useful for the coaching team?

**Head Coach:** Reports such as member attendance, workout progress, active training sessions, instructor schedules, VIP session enrollments, and membership status would be useful. Revenue reports covering memberships, VIP sessions, and shop sales would help management monitor performance and improve decision-making. Visitor inquiries submitted through the website should also be accessible to administration for follow-up.

---

**Scrum Master:** Are there any operational areas outside direct coaching that should still be part of the same system?

**Head Coach:** Yes. A gym shop with inventory and point-of-sale billing should be included so that front-desk staff can sell supplements or merchandise and update stock automatically. Admin staff should also be able to register walk-in members, renew memberships at the gym, upload instructor photos for the website, and manage membership packages. These functions support daily operations and should work together with member and instructor activities in one system.

---

**Scrum Master:** Finally, what are your main expectations from the new ERP system as the Head Coach?

**Head Coach:** I expect the system to simplify training management by centralizing instructor activities, workout plans, member progress, and session scheduling. It should help coaches provide a better service while improving the overall efficiency of Roar Fitness operations. The member portal, instructor portal, admin portal, and public website should all reflect the same up-to-date information so that we operate as one integrated organization.

---

**Scrum Master:** Thank you for sharing your valuable insights. The information collected from this interview will help us identify the system requirements and design features that support instructors and improve training operations. If we need any further clarification during the analysis phase, we will contact you.

**Head Coach:** You're welcome. I look forward to seeing the proposed solution and how it improves the operations of Roar Fitness.

**Scrum Master:** Thank you again for your time and cooperation. Have a great day.

**Head Coach:** Thank you. Have a great day as well.
